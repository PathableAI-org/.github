# PathAble

## Engineering at PathAble

PathAble builds practical, accessible workflow software with disability employment and coaching organizations.

We help staff guide sessions, capture notes, create documentation and reporting artifacts, and preserve operational memory. Our technology is designed to strengthen human support, not replace human judgment, relationships, or agency.

### What we work on

- Staff-facing coaching and session workflows
- Structured note capture and AI-assisted summaries
- Compliance-aware documentation and reporting
- Accessible, mobile-first software
- Human-directed AI for high-trust environments
- Reusable workflow primitives and open-source tools

### How we build

**Workflow first.** We begin with the real work, decisions, handoffs, evidence, and exceptions experienced by frontline staff.

**Augment, do not replace.** People retain meaningful control, responsibility, and judgment.

**Accessibility is infrastructure.** Accessibility shapes architecture, interaction design, content, testing, and delivery.

**Built with communities.** We learn from practitioners and disabled people throughout discovery and development.

**Accountable by design.** We prioritize privacy, auditability, minimum-necessary access, and candid communication about limitations.

### Building in public

This organization is also where we are developing our public engineering practice. We plan to share:

- Field notes from product and engineering work
- Architecture decisions and tradeoffs
- Accessible engineering practices
- Patterns for responsible AI-assisted workflows
- Small, reusable open-source building blocks
- Retrospectives on what worked, what failed, and what changed

Public examples use synthetic data. They must never include protected health information, participant information, customer data, or confidential implementation details.
